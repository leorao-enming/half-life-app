# Half-Life product direction — 2026

## Product promise

**Today’s focus without sacrificing tonight’s sleep.**

Half-Life is a caffeine-curfew coach, not a general health dashboard or a coffee scrapbook. It helps people decide whether to have a drink now and understand when caffeine is likely to have a lower impact on their sleep.

## Differentiated visual system

One system, four layers:

1. **Sip Trace** — an abstract, time-decaying trace created for every drink.
2. **Night Map** — the day’s traces combine into a clear low-impact window.
3. **Caffeine Weather** — one plain-language summary of the evening.
4. **Curfew Stamp** — a static history token, not a cutout sticker.

Drink photos are private input and optional memory thumbnails. They must not become white-outline cutout stickers, a coffee calendar, character mascots, or branded cup illustrations. The first-party graphic language is limited to original rings, fade curves, ripples, arcs, pulse bars, and orbit dots.

## Product principles

- Lead with a decision, then the explanation, then the data.
- Say “estimated” and state the data source; do not make medical claims.
- Keep one prominent action per screen.
- Use photography to make logging personal; use traces to make the product useful.
- Never expose developer, backend, or configuration status in a user-facing screen.

## The four primary destinations

| Destination | User question | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tonight | Can I have caffeine now? | A low-impact time and Night Map. |
| Record | What did I drink? | Confirmed dose, impact preview, and a Sip Trace. |
| Plan | What does tomorrow require? | A wake-up/deadline-aware caffeine curfew. |
| My patterns | What have I learned? | Personal late-caffeine patterns after enough data. |

## 8.5 quality gates

The product is not considered 8.5/10 until all gates pass:

- A target user can locate tonight’s answer within 10 seconds.
- A drink can be logged in 8 seconds or less using a preset or photo confirmation.
- Body text is at least 17pt; auxiliary text is at least 13pt; interactive targets are at least 44pt.
- Every prediction says it is an estimate, gives a plain-language explanation, and does not imply diagnosis.
- No user-facing screen contains configuration, backend, or developer copy.
- Five target users complete “check tonight”, “record a drink”, and “find why” with at least 90% task success.

## Non-goals for the repositioning

- Competing with broad recovery, nutrition, or training dashboards.
- Coffee-shop discovery, social feeds, ratings, or a beverage scrapbook.
- Medical sleep diagnoses or medication advice.
